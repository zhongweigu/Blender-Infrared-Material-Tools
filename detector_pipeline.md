### 4.3.3. Radiation of detector's direction

After getting self-radiation and reflection radiation of the target, the target radiation model of the zero distance in a hemisphere space is expressed as:

$$ L = \varepsilon L_{self} + \frac{\rho E_i}{\pi} \tag{16} $$

$L$ is the radiant energy emitted by the target to the entire hemisphere space. The detector is observed from a particular location of the aircraft, and therefore the angle $\theta_s$, which is the angle between the normal vector of each vertex of the target and the connection line from the detector to the vertex, is needed to be calculated. So does the angle $\theta_c$ which is the angle between the connection line from the detector to the vertex and the detector's line of sight. Finally the target's IR value of zero distance per unit area from the detector's direction can be obtained in Eq. (17).

$$ L_{cam-direction} = L \cdot \cos\theta_c \cdot \cos\theta_s \tag{17} $$
